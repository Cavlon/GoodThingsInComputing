AI Reduces Risk of Surgical Complications

An AI created by Dr. Amin Madani, a general surgeon at the Sprott Department of Surgery at the University Health Network, Toronto, helps to identify tissue that is safe to dissect during surgery.

According to research by the World Health Organization, 1 out of every 4 inpatients suffer complications during or after a surgery. It can be difficult for a surgeon to identify safe areas of organ to dissect, especially when performing Laparoscopy (keyhole surgery), where they can only see into the body via a camera and screen. However, Dr Madani, inspired by the techniques used by top surgeons, thought an AI may be able to help visualise the safe zones. He trained the AI on hundreds of hours of gallbladder surgeries, and feedback from expert surgeons on where they would dissect, and where they thought a dissection may cause a bleed. The AI highlights "go" and "no-go" areas of the surgical field for bisection, in green and red respectively; Dr. Madani also has a version with a heatmap, showing how certain the AI is of safety.

Although GoNoGoNet can currently only assist on gallbladder surgeries, the AI could recognise "go" and "no-go" zones with 93-95% accuracy. This has the potential to significantly decrease the risk of major bile duct injuries in Laparoscopic Cholecystectomies and certainly shows a promising proof of concept, which could be applied toall kinds of surgical operations.
